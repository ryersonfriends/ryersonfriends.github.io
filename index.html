THE GOAL

Bridging together the student community

As a student, a big part of what makes going to Ryerson memorable is the people you meet. But it can be particularly challenging to meet new friends at a university where most of the students commute in and out of campus. Our goal is to help build a stronger student community and help foster friendships at every intersection of your education.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Join our community on slack

2. Hang out in the #friends channel

There are also neighbourhood specific channels like #markham-friends, #etobicoke-friends, #mississauga-friends etc.

3. Get paired with a new student every week

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
What is slack and donut?

Slack is a messaging platform used for group communication. We're using the Donut app for Slack to help introduce students who don't know each other well and to encourage them to meet up for coffee or lunch. You'll be messaged at the beginning of every week along with another student to organize a hang out together.

What was the motivation behind this idea?

As current and former students, we feel we have a good understanding of how difficult it is to sometimes find ways to get involved on campus, especially if you don't live on residence. And frankly, you miss out on meeting a lot of great people when you're not on campus for events. We're looking to bridge that gap and help build friendships throughout the school year and beyond.

How are you associated with Ryerson University?

We're Ryerson students and alumni looking to make a positive impact in the Ryerson community. Outside of being students and sharing similar values, we are not associated with Ryerson University in any other way.
