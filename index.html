THE GOAL

Bridging together the student community

As a student, going to Ryerson is more than just the courses you take, it's about the people you meet. But it can be particularly challenging to meet new friends at a university that has a large student population commuting in and out of campus. Our goal is to help build a stronger student community, fostering friendships at every intersection of your education.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Join our community on slack

2. Hang out in the #friends channel

There are also neighbourhood specific channels like #markham-friends, #etobicoke-friends, #mississauga-friends etc.

3. Get paired with a new student every week

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
What is slack and donut?

Slack is a messaging platform typically used for workplace communication. We've extended its use for building a student community. It also has a ton of apps that integrate with it. We're using the Donut app for Slack which helps introduce students who don't know each other well and encourages them to meet up for coffee, lunch or well...donuts.

What was the motivation behind this idea?

As former students, we felt we had a good understanding of how difficult it is to sometimes find ways to get involved on campus, especially if you don't live on residence. And frankly, you miss out on meeting a lot of great people when you're not on campus for events. We're looking to bridge that gap and help you build friendships throughout the school year and beyond.

How are you associated with Ryerson University?

We're a group of Ryerson Alumni looking to make a positive impact in the Ryerson community. Outside of being graduates and sharing similar values, we are not associated with Ryerson University in any other way.
